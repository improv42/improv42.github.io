About

It was night watch at the busiest ambulance service in the United States, in one of its most violent cities.

...and I was a new Paramedic.

I learned really quickly that your technology not only has to function the way you'd expect, EVERY time, but it also has to be intuitive. It doesn't matter what it can do, if you can't figure out how to do it.

One of the largest Paramedic schools in the United States, and the only one that used technology at its core for all of its teaching.

...could you run the New Orleans Campus for us?

Over the long term, and over long distances, technology can be life saving, and enlightening. It can also present great troubleshooting opportunities. Managing an entire campus as well as its technology is a great opportunity to see how far you can stretch.

A busy private ambulance service, required to adopt new technology that will affect every moving part of its business.

...could you handle that for us, please?

Upgrading to computer documentation changes everything from the Paramedic's patient interaction to the billing process at the end. There are new technologies and equipment to maintain and troubleshoot, as well as teaching the entire company how to use them. Management of large scale, long term projects is an art that involves creating a harmony between implementation, education, and maintenance.

But, what does this mean for you?

Having worked as a Paramedic, Teacher, Manager, and Technology Coordinator, I understand the user side of software and its implementation.

As a full stack Software Developer, I can help you create and implement your solution in a way that works for you and all of your people.
